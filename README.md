# 2023-Affordable-Housing-Research-Analysis
This project analyzes the 2023 Affordable Housing Dataset from NYC Open Data to evaluate the city’s progress in addressing the housing crisis. By examining over [Insert Total Number of Rows] projects, this analysis explores where housing is actually being built, who can afford it, and how long it takes to deliver units to the market.


# Key Questions Answered
* **Affordability Tiers:** What percentage of 'affordable' units actually serve the city's most vulnerable (ELI/VLI) populations?
* **The Family Gap**: Are we building enough family-sized (3-BR+) housing, or is the market oversaturated with studios?
* **Geographic Equity**: Which boroughs and neighborhoods (NTAs) are leading in housing production vs. those that are falling behind?
Development Efficiency: What is the average 'time-to-market' from project financing to building completion?

#**Questions I Asked**:
* **The Borough Leaderboard (Construction Type)**: Which borough saw the most New Construction in 2023, and what was the ratio of New Construction vs. Preservation for each borough?
* **The "Deep Affordability" Check**: What percentage of all units counted in 2023 were dedicated to Extremely Low Income (ELI) and Very Low Income (VLI) households?
* **The Family Gap**: What is the citywide percentage of family-sized units (3-BR or larger), and which borough is doing the best job providing for actual families?
* **The Efficiency Metric**: What was the average time-to-completion (in months) for affordable housing projects finished in 2023?
* **The "New vs. Old" Ratio**: How does the total volume of New Construction (adding new supply) compare to Preservation (maintaining existing supply) across the city?
* **The Homeownership Opportunity**: What percentage of the 2023 housing "wins" were for Homeownership (buying) versus Rentals?
* **The Neighborhood Hotspots**: Which specific Neighborhood Tabulation Areas (NTAs) saw the highest concentration of units, and are they clustered in specific areas?
* **The Middle Income Reality Check**: What percentage of "affordable" units were actually designated for Middle Income households ($100k+ earners)?
